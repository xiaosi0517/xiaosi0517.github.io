# Research Methodology & Verification Guide

**How This Candidate List Was Built**

---

## Search Strategy Overview

This research was conducted to find **realistic, reachable** young professors suitable for part-time external ML research collaboration. The methodology focused on:

1. **Non-elite institutions** (China 211 universities, US mid-tier)
2. **Early-career researchers** (3-6 years post-PhD, or 2-4 years on faculty)
3. **Systems/engineering-focused** research (not pure theory)
4. **Active publishers** in target areas (2023-2026 publication record)
5. **Clear collaboration signals** (infrastructure needs, engineering-heavy work)

## Selection Criteria Applied

### INCLUSION Criteria (✅ Candidates included if meeting these):

**Career Stage:**
- Joined faculty between 2018-2024 (✅)
- OR 3-7 years post-PhD (✅)
- OR Assistant/early Associate Professor level (✅)

**Research Activity:**
- Published 2+ papers in target areas during 2023-2026 (✅)
- In top-tier venues (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, ICCV, ICRA, ACL) (✅)
- OR strong publications in good secondary venues (EMNLP, ICCV, ECCV, AAAI, IJCAI, IJARS) (✅)

**Research Focus:**
- Systems/engineering-heavy (infrastructure, deployment, benchmarking) (✅)
- Applied AI orientation (not pure theory/math) (✅)
- Target areas: LLM, multimodal, VLA, efficient inference, AI systems (✅)

**Institutional Fit:**
- For China: 211 university or non-985 with strong reputation (✅)
- For US: Mid-tier university (not top 5) (✅)
- For international: Strong research institution with collaboration track record (✅)

**Collaboration Signals:**
- Multiple collaborators on recent papers (✅)
- Evidence of external partnerships (✅)
- Open-source code or shared datasets (✅)
- Lab website with engagement info (✅)

### EXCLUSION Criteria (❌ Candidates excluded if meeting these):

- ❌ Very senior or famous professor (20+ years faculty)
- ❌ At elite institutions (Stanford, MIT, CMU, Berkeley, Princeton for US)
- ❌ Pure theoretical research with no systems/applications
- ❌ Few recent publications (appears inactive or shifting away)
- ❌ No collaboration signals (works alone, no open science)
- ❌ Highly restrictive institutional policies (some military labs, closed corporate)
- ❌ No traceable contact information or web presence

## Verification Checklist (For You to Validate)

Before reaching out to any candidate, verify:

### Quick Verification (10 min per candidate):

- [ ] **Faculty page exists** on university website (shows current status)
- [ ] **Recent email accessible** (university directory or faculty page)
- [ ] **Google Scholar profile active** (shows they're publishing)
- [ ] **Recent papers exist** (search for their name + "2024" or "2025")
- [ ] **Lab website has updates** from last 6 months (shows active work)

### Deeper Verification (15 min per candidate):

- [ ] **Paper titles match research areas** (spot-check recent papers)
- [ ] **Co-authors diverse** (shows collaboration capability)
- [ ] **Publication venue credible** (check conference ranking)
- [ ] **Early-career status confirmed** (check join date, PhD year)
- [ ] **Research area still active** (not shifted to different focus)
- [ ] **Lab size reasonable** (not mega-lab, not solo researcher)
- [ ] **Funding visible** (project/grant websites suggest active research)

### Comprehensive Verification (optional, 30 min):

- [ ] **PhD advisor identified** (helps with research trajectory)
- [ ] **Postdoc path checked** (shows research evolution)
- [ ] **Citations recent** (papers are being cited, not forgotten)
- [ ] **GitHub activity actual** (not just old code, but recent commits)
- [ ] **Collaboration history** (can you find evidence of external collaboration?)
- [ ] **Interview/talk history** (has given talks, presented at workshops—good sign)
- [ ] **Media mentions** (news or blog posts about their work—visibility)


## How to Validate This Research

### Cross-Reference Each Candidate:

1. **Google Scholar + University Directory Triangulation**
   - Does Google Scholar profile match university listing?
   - Are recent papers attributed to the same institution?
   - Is affiliation consistent over time?

2. **ArXiv + Conference Database Cross-Check**
   - Find recent ArXiv papers by the researcher
   - Check if they eventually appeared in conference proceedings
   - Verify author institution consistency

3. **Lab Website + Publication Record Alignment**
   - Lab website lists projects
   - Do recent papers match project descriptions?
   - Are co-authors from lab listed?

4. **Collaboration Signal Verification**
   - Find a recent paper by candidate
   - Check co-author affiliations
   - Verify external collaborators (not same institution)
   - Look for multi-institution papers (good sign)

## Known Limitations of This Research

### Information Sources:
- **Public information only** (no private communications or unpublished data)
- **English-language sources** (some Chinese academics may have limited English presence)
- **Recently published only** (2023-2026 bias, may miss emerging researchers)
- **Traceable online only** (researchers without web presence missed)

### Verification Challenges:
- **Name ambiguity** (common Chinese surnames may match multiple researchers)
- **Affiliation changes** (may not be immediately updated on university sites)
- **Research area shifts** (junior faculty sometimes pivot, outdated directory listings)
- **Email format uncertainty** (university email patterns vary)
- **Publication lag** (conferences accept papers months before publication)

### Geographic Biases:
- **English-language bias** (Chinese researchers with limited English presence under-represented)
- **Tier-1 university bias** (more web presence, easier to find)
- **Publication venue bias** (researchers publishing in local conferences less visible)
- **China mainland bias** (Hong Kong/Taiwan researchers better represented in English sources)

## Recommended Verification Workflow

Before sending outreach emails:

1. **Batch Verification (30 min for all top contacts):**
   - Check Google Scholar for each candidate
   - Verify faculty page + email
   - Scan 2-3 recent papers (titles/abstracts only)
   - Note any concerns or outdated information

2. **Individual Email Customization (10 min per candidate):**
   - Pick one specific recent paper
   - Read abstract + introduction
   - Write custom pitch mentioning that specific work
   - Include paper title in subject line for credibility

3. **Final Checks Before Send:**
   - Email address verified (test if possible)
   - Candidate still at listed institution (no recent moves)
   - Research area matches your expertise
   - Collaboration likelihood assessment still valid
