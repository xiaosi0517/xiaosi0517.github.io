Developed transparent graphene-based microelectrode arrays that enable simultaneous electrical recording and optical stimulation of retinal tissue. This work demonstrated the unique advantages of graphene's optical transparency for retinal electrophysiology studies.

**Key publications:**
- X. Zhang et al., "Probing light-stimulated activities in the retina via transparent graphene electrodes," *ACS Applied Bio Materials*, 2022.
- A. Esteban-Linares, X. Zhang et al., "Graphene-based microfluidic perforated microelectrode arrays for retinal electrophysiological studies," *Lab on a Chip*, 2023.
